## Executive Summary

This is the zero-budget marketing playbook. Not "low budget" -- zero. The Cuban doctrine: if the product needs paid ads to grow, the product is not good enough yet. Fix the product, not the funnel.

Niotebook's earned media strategy has five channels, sequenced in order of execution: (1) Product Hunt launch, (2) Hacker News Show HN, (3) press/media outreach to edtech publications, (4) influencer/creator partnerships with CS education YouTubers, and (5) ongoing community-driven virality through a product that students naturally share.

The combined expected impact of a well-executed earned media blitz: 10,000-30,000 website visits and 1,000-5,000 signups in the first 30 days post-launch. Cost: zero dollars. Investment: 2-3 weeks of Akram's focused time.

Our unit economics make this viable: at $0.54/user/month all-in cost (from CFO board report), we can afford to serve thousands of free users while the product-led growth flywheel spins up. Competitors with $3-5/user server costs cannot do this.

## 1. Zero-Budget Marketing Philosophy

### Why Zero Budget Is a Strategy, Not a Limitation

Niotebook's cost per user is $0.54/month. Competitors with server-side execution spend $3-5/user/month. This means we can afford to grow slower than them and still survive -- because every free user costs us almost nothing. We have the luxury of patience. And patience, combined with a product students actually want, creates earned growth that compounds forever.

Mark Cuban built Cost Plus Drugs to 2 million customers with literally zero marketing spend. The product was so obviously valuable -- transparent drug pricing -- that customers became the sales force. Niotebook needs to be that obvious for CS students: "Why would I tab-switch when I can use this?"

### The Product-Led Growth Flywheel

```
Student discovers Niotebook (earned media)
  -> Tries it (free, zero friction)
    -> Experiences the "aha moment" (Nio knows the lecture context)
      -> Tells classmate / posts about it
        -> Classmate discovers Niotebook (organic)
          -> Flywheel accelerates
```

The flywheel only works if the "aha moment" is real. Before any launch activity, validate that new users consistently reach the aha moment within their first session. **The aha moment for Niotebook: the first time a student asks Nio a question and gets a response that references what the professor just said in the lecture.**

### Zero-Budget Marketing Principles

1. **Product is the marketing.** Every dollar spent on ads is a dollar admitting the product is not good enough.
2. **Authenticity scales.** A genuine founder story resonates more than a polished launch video.
3. **Help first, pitch never.** Be the most helpful person in r/cs50. Let the product speak for itself.
4. **One launch, multiple platforms.** Coordinate timing so Product Hunt, Hacker News, Reddit, and Twitter all amplify each other in the same 48-hour window.
5. **Earn press, do not chase it.** The "solo developer builds 7 WASM runtimes in one browser tab" story writes itself.

## 2. Product Hunt Launch Plan

### 2.1 Why Product Hunt

Product Hunt provides legitimacy ("featured on Product Hunt" badge), a burst of traffic from early adopters, and long-tail discoverability. The 2025-2026 Product Hunt landscape has changed significantly: only featured products get meaningful traffic. The "Featured" vs "All" tab distinction means getting featured is the single most important success factor.

Key 2025-2026 PH insight: 400+ engaged supporters from established accounts far outweigh 4,000 cold contacts. The algorithm detects coordinated upvote campaigns aggressively.

### 2.2 Pre-Launch Preparation (3-4 Weeks Before)

**Week -4: Account and community prep**
- Akram's Product Hunt account should be active: upvote products, leave thoughtful comments, follow relevant makers.
- Identify 5-10 Product Hunt power users in the edtech/dev tools space. Engage with their launches. Build genuine relationships.
- Create and publish the product's "Upcoming" page on Product Hunt. Encourage alpha users to subscribe.

**Week -3: Asset creation**
- **Tagline for PH:** "Video lectures + live code + AI tutor. One tab." (under 60 characters)
- **Description:** 300-word description covering the problem, solution, key features, and who it is for.
- **Media gallery (5-6 items):**
  1. Three-pane workspace overview (hero shot, dark mode)
  2. Nio responding with lecture context (showing timestamp reference)
  3. C code compiling in browser via Wasmer
  4. Python running via Pyodide with CS50 library
  5. Course catalog showing CS50 course variants
  6. GIF: 15-second demo of the full workflow (watch -> code -> ask Nio)
- **Product video:** Video 1 (hero video) from content strategy, under 2 minutes.
- **Logo:** Niotebook wordmark, high resolution, light and dark variants.

**Week -2: First comment drafting**
The first comment (maker's comment) is the most important piece of copy in the entire launch. Draft and refine it.

Structure:
```
Line 1: Personal story.
"I was a CS student who spent more time setting up tools than learning."

Line 2-3: The problem.
"7.1 million students take CS50 online. They all tab-switch between YouTube,
VS Code, and ChatGPT. None of these tools know about each other."

Line 4-5: The solution.
"Niotebook puts the lecture, a code editor, and an AI tutor in one tab.
The AI knows what the professor just said -- it reads the lecture transcript
and sees your code and errors in real time."

Line 6: Technical credibility.
"7 WASM runtimes (C, Python, JS, HTML/CSS, SQL, R), all client-side.
Zero server containers. Cost per user: $0.54/month."

Line 7: Call to action.
"Try it free at niotebook.com. Feedback in this thread goes directly
to me -- I am the entire team."
```

Keep it under 800 characters. Every word earns its place.

**Week -1: Outreach and coordination**
- Send personal messages to 20-30 people who might engage: alpha users, friends in tech, people met through community engagement.
- DO NOT ask for upvotes. Ask: "I am launching Niotebook on Product Hunt next [day]. Would love your honest feedback."
- Prepare tweets and LinkedIn posts for launch day.
- Confirm all links work, signup flow is smooth, product is stable. One crash on launch day kills everything.

### 2.3 Launch Day Execution

**Timing:** Launch on Tuesday or Wednesday. Post goes live at 12:01 AM Pacific. Tuesday-Wednesday maximizes the chance of being featured by the PH team (lower competition than Thursday-Friday).

| Time (PT) | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| 12:01 AM | Product goes live. Post first comment immediately. |
| 12:05 AM | Share with Akram's immediate network via personal messages. Not mass blasts. |
| 7:00 AM | Cross-post to Twitter/X and LinkedIn. Share in Discord. |

## 3. Hacker News Launch Strategy

### 3.1 Why Hacker News

Hacker News is the single highest-quality traffic source for developer-facing products. A front-page Show HN post averages 5,000-50,000 views and attracts technically sophisticated early adopters who give the most valuable feedback. The HN audience values technical depth, honesty, and solo-developer stories -- exactly Niotebook's strengths.

### 3.2 Pre-Launch (Weeks Before)

- Akram should be an active HN commenter for 4+ weeks before launching. Comment on AI-in-education threads, WASM posts, edtech discussions, solo developer stories.
- Build karma organically (need 5+ comment karma to post without being flagged).
- Study successful edtech/dev tool Show HN posts for title and comment patterns.

### 3.3 The Show HN Post

**Title:** "Show HN: Niotebook -- Video lectures + code editor + AI tutor in one browser tab"

Title must be under 80 characters, descriptive, and avoid buzzwords. "AI-powered" is a red flag on HN. Focus on what it does, not how it does it.

**Post body (text):**

```
I built Niotebook because I was tired of tab-switching between YouTube, VS Code,
and ChatGPT while studying CS50.

Niotebook puts the lecture video, a multi-language code editor, and a
context-aware AI tutor in one browser tab. The AI reads the lecture transcript at
the current timestamp and sees your code and errors -- so when you ask "what did
the professor mean about pointers?", it gives you an answer grounded in the
actual lecture, not a generic ChatGPT response.

Technical details:
- 7 in-browser language runtimes via WASM (C/Wasmer, Python/Pyodide, JS,
  HTML/CSS, SQL/sql.js, R/webR) -- zero server-side execution
- Context builder assembles video timestamp + transcript window + code snapshot +
  runtime errors into a single AI prompt with token budget management
- Dual AI provider: Gemini primary, Groq fallback with first-token timeout
  detection
- Built with Next.js 16, React 19, TypeScript, Convex, CodeMirror 6
- All execution is client-side. Cost per user: ~$0.54/month

Currently curates CS50 courses (CS50x, CS50P, CS50W, CS50AI, CS50SQL, CS50R)
with course-specific environment presets (cs50.h header support, starter files,
compiler flags).

Solo developer project. Free to use. Would love your feedback.

niotebook.com
```

### 3.4 HN Timing

- Post early in the week: Monday or Tuesday, 8-10 AM Eastern (best for US + Europe overlap).
- Do NOT launch on the same day as Product Hunt. Stagger by 1-2 days so each gets dedicated attention.
- Ideal sequence: **Product Hunt on Tuesday, Hacker News on Thursday.**

### 3.5 HN Engagement Rules

1. Respond to every comment, especially critical ones. HN rewards founders who engage thoughtfully with criticism.
2. Be honest about limitations: "You are right, we only support CS50 courses right now. Expanding to MIT OCW is the next priority."
3. Technical questions get technical answers. Show the architecture. Link to specific code decisions.
4. NEVER ask friends to upvote. HN detects and penalizes vote rings aggressively.
5. If the post does not gain traction, try again next week with a different angle (e.g., focus on the WASM runtime story instead of the education angle).
6. Prepare for the "this is just a feature" criticism. Have a thoughtful answer about the vision (open learning runtime, course pack spec, protocol not product).

### 3.6 Hacker News Success Metrics

| Metric | Minimum | Good | Great |
|--------|---------|------|-------|
| Points | 50 | 150 | 400+ |
| Comments | 20 | 50 | 100+ |
| Front page time | 1 hour | 4 hours | 12+ hours |
| Website visits | 2,000 | 10,000 | 30,000+ |
| Signups | 200 | 1,000 | 3,000+ |

### 5.2 Special Case: CS50 / David Malan Outreach

This is the most sensitive and most valuable outreach target. CS50 is our primary content source. David Malan is the most recognizable CS educator in the world. An endorsement from him would be transformative. A rejection could be devastating (especially if CS50's academic honesty policy explicitly prohibits Niotebook).

**Outreach approach:**
1. **Research first.** Understand CS50's academic honesty policy (it prohibits "AI-based software other than CS50's own" for submitted work). Position Niotebook as a study companion, not a tool for completing graded assignments.
2. **Lead with respect.** Acknowledge CS50 Duck and the CS50 team's own tools. Position as complementary, never competitive.
3. **Emphasize Socratic design.** Our `nioPrompt.ts` explicitly refuses to give complete solutions to graded tasks. This aligns with CS50's academic integrity goals.
4. **Offer value.** "We would love to integrate with CS50 Duck or follow your guidelines for third-party tools."
5. **Be prepared for rejection.** If CS50 says no, we still serve CS50 content (it is Creative Commons licensed on YouTube). But we must diversify to reduce this dependency.

### 5.4 Creator Partnership Principles

1. **Never pay for mentions.** If they do not find it genuinely useful, a paid mention will ring hollow.
2. **Make it easy to try.** Create a special link for each creator that pre-loads a relevant course/lesson so they experience the aha moment immediately (e.g., niotebook.com/demo?course=cs50x&week=1).
3. **Respect their time.** Keep outreach short. One follow-up maximum.
4. **Celebrate any mention.** Amplify their content. Build genuine relationships, not transactional ones.
5. **Provide assets.** If they want to feature Niotebook, have screenshots, GIFs, and a fact sheet ready.

## 8. What We Will NOT Do

1. **We will NOT pay for any influencer mention, press placement, or social media promotion.** Zero dollars means zero dollars.
2. **We will NOT spam communities with product links.** One bad-faith Reddit post can permanently damage our reputation in communities we need.
3. **We will NOT fake user testimonials or reviews.** Every quote, every review, every mention must be genuine.
4. **We will NOT launch before the product is ready.** One chance at a first impression. The aha moment must work reliably before any launch activity.
5. **We will NOT chase vanity metrics.** 10,000 Product Hunt upvotes mean nothing if no one comes back after Day 1. Optimize for Day-7 retention, not launch-day numbers.
6. **We will NOT ask anyone to upvote.** We ask for feedback, not votes. The algorithm and the community both punish vote manipulation.
