## Executive Summary

Distribution is the game. The product is built. Seven WASM runtimes work. Nio reads lecture transcripts. The three-pane workspace exists. Now the question is: how do 7.1 million CS50 students (and millions more taking free CS courses worldwide) find out about it?

This document ranks every distribution channel by expected impact and cost, maps the CS student community landscape with real subscriber/member counts, outlines a university CS department outreach plan, defines a browser extension strategy, and identifies platform partnership opportunities.

The core insight from the board meeting: **we are racing the clock.** Google, Scrimba, and Coursera are all building toward our value proposition from different directions. We have 12-18 months before an incumbent ships something close enough to make our pitch irrelevant. Every channel in this document is evaluated on one criterion: how fast can it deliver students who reach the aha moment?

Total budget for all channels: zero dollars. Total investment: Akram's time and the quality of the product itself.

### 3.3 Outreach Strategy

**Phase 1 (Months 1-3): Research and warm introductions**

1. Identify the right person at each university: the professor teaching intro CS, the head of the CS education committee, or the director of educational technology.
2. Research their current tooling. What IDE do they recommend? What AI policy do they have? What pain points have they mentioned in published talks or tweets?
3. Find warm introductions: alumni connections, conference contacts, Twitter/academic Twitter engagement.

**Phase 2 (Months 3-6): Direct outreach**

Email template for professors:

```

Akram
```

For universities that express interest:

2. Offer to present at a department meeting or TA training session
3. Provide a feedback channel for their students
4. Report back with anonymized usage data and student feedback

## 4. Browser Extension Strategy

### 4.1 The Vision

A Chrome/Firefox extension that detects when a student is watching a CS lecture on YouTube and offers a one-click "Open in Niotebook" button. This captures students at the exact moment they need us -- watching a lecture and wanting to code along.

### 4.2 Why This Matters

YouTube is where CS students already are. CS50 alone has tens of millions of views on YouTube. MIT OCW, Stanford, Computerphile, freeCodeCamp -- billions of combined views. A browser extension that activates on these videos creates a distribution channel with zero ongoing cost and high intent.

### 4.3 Extension Features (MVP)

1. **YouTube detection:** Detect CS lecture videos on YouTube (by channel, playlist, or keyword matching)
2. **"Open in Niotebook" button:** Overlay a small, non-intrusive button on detected videos
3. **Auto-course matching:** If the video belongs to a known course (CS50, MIT OCW), automatically match it to the correct Niotebook course/lesson
4. **Quick start for unknown videos:** For unrecognized CS videos, offer to open in a generic sandbox environment
5. **Transcript extraction:** Pre-fetch YouTube transcript data to provide to Nio

### 4.4 Extension Technical Spec

- **Platform:** Chrome Web Store (primary), Firefox Add-ons (secondary)
- **Technology:** Manifest V3, content scripts on youtube.com
- **Detection logic:** Match video against known course playlists + keyword detection in titles/descriptions
- **Privacy:** Zero data collection. All processing happens locally. No tracking.
- **Size:** Minimal footprint. No heavy dependencies.

### 4.5 Extension Distribution Strategy

1. **Chrome Web Store:** Free listing. Optimize title and description for "CS50 study tool" and "coding lecture companion" searches.
2. **Cross-promote:** Mention the extension in all 5 product videos, all blog posts, and the landing page.
3. **YouTube video descriptions:** When creating CS50-related YouTube content, link to the extension.
4. **Reddit/community:** Share in r/cs50 when the extension launches.

### 4.6 Extension Timeline

| Phase | Scope | Timeline | Effort |
|-------|-------|----------|--------|
| Design & prototype | Manifest V3 content script, YouTube detection, basic UI | Month 3-4 | 2 weeks |
| MVP development | Core features, CS50 playlist matching, "Open in Niotebook" button | Month 4-5 | 3 weeks |
| Testing & launch | Chrome Web Store review, Firefox port, community launch | Month 5-6 | 2 weeks |
| Iteration | Usage analytics, new course detection, user feedback | Month 6+ | Ongoing |

## 5. Platform Partnerships

### 5.1 YouTube Learning Partnership

**What it is:** YouTube has been investing in educational content through YouTube Learning and YouTube Courses. A partnership could mean featuring Niotebook as a recommended learning tool for CS courses on YouTube.

**Why it matters:** YouTube is THE distribution channel for CS lecture content. Being officially endorsed or integrated would give us access to the 7.1M+ CS50 viewers and millions more watching MIT OCW, Stanford, and freeCodeCamp videos.

**What we offer YouTube:** A tool that makes their educational content more engaging and sticky. Students who use Niotebook alongside YouTube lectures spend more time on YouTube (they stay on the video while coding along). We improve YouTube's educational value proposition.

**Outreach approach:**
1. Start with the YouTube Education team (youtube.com/learning)
2. Pitch: "We make CS lecture videos on YouTube more interactive by adding a code editor and AI tutor that reads the transcript"
3. Demo the product with a CS50 lecture
4. Ask for: featured listing in YouTube Learning, API access for better transcript integration

**Timeline:** This is a long-term play (6-12 months). Build traction first, then approach YouTube with usage data.

### 5.2 LMS Integration Partnership

**What it is:** Integrate Niotebook into Learning Management Systems (Canvas, Moodle, Blackboard) via LTI (Learning Tools Interoperability) standard.

**Why it matters:** LMS is where university courses live. If a professor can add Niotebook as a tool within Canvas, it becomes part of the official course workflow. This is the highest-intent distribution channel possible: mandatory course tools.

**What we offer LMS providers:** An embeddable interactive coding widget that works with any video content. Reduces the need for separate coding environments. Improves student engagement metrics.

**Technical requirements:**
- LTI 1.3 integration (industry standard for LMS tools)
- Deep linking support (link directly to specific course/lesson)
- Grade passback (if we add assessment features)
- SSO via university identity provider

**Outreach targets:**
1. **Canvas (Instructure):** Largest US higher-ed LMS. Canvas App Center for discovery.
2. **Moodle:** Open-source LMS, 100,000+ sites worldwide. Plugin marketplace opening in 2026.
3. **Open LMS:** Moodle-based, education-focused. Partnership ecosystem includes AI tools (TeacherMatic, Copyleaks).
4. **Blackboard (Anthology):** Legacy but still widely used.

**Timeline:** LTI integration requires engineering investment. Target Month 9-12 for MVP.

### 5.3 Open Courseware Provider Partnerships

**What it is:** Official partnerships with MIT OCW, Stanford Engineering Everywhere, and other open courseware providers to be listed as a recommended companion tool.

**Why it matters:** An official recommendation from MIT OCW or Stanford would be the ultimate social proof. It reduces the "who are you?" barrier completely.

**Outreach approach:** See Partnership Playbook document for detailed strategy.

## 7. What We Will NOT Do

1. **We will NOT spend money on paid distribution channels (Google Ads, Facebook Ads, sponsored posts).** If the product needs paid distribution, the product is not ready.
2. **We will NOT spam any community.** One bad-faith post destroys months of relationship building.
3. **We will NOT pursue distribution channels that require content we do not have.** No fake university partnerships, no manufactured testimonials.
4. **We will NOT build the browser extension before we have 500+ active users.** Validate the product first, then extend distribution.
5. **We will NOT pursue LMS integration before we have at least one university pilot.** Build the relationship first, then invest in technical integration.

---

*Distribution is not about being everywhere. It is about being in the right place, at the right time, with the right message, for the right person. A CS student stuck on pointers at 2am, searching Reddit for help -- that is our person. Be there for them. The rest follows.*
